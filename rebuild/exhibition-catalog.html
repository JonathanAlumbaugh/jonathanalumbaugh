for an art history exhibition Jonathan designed An exhibition catalog is created to supplement the (often) abridged information found in an exhibit. The exhibition catalog consisted of 11 essays by class-members of ARH 400 that delved into the artist/influencer's lives. Due to class budget, the catalog was limited to 28 pages, including front and back. The catalog was to be printed on 100% post-consumer waste paper. It is important to place historical figures in context. By placing a historical figure the reader gains a better understanding of the figures' nuanced and lasting influence on history. The table of contents was the one place which all artists would be seen together. It was the best chance to give historical context to each artists' life. Three layouts were developed to accomodate essays of different lengths: two essays to a spread, with a small photo; one essay to a spread with a full bleed opposite; and one essay to a spread with references and a 1/3 page photo opposite.
